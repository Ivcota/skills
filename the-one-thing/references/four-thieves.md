# The Four Thieves of Productivity (Chapter 17)

All citations: Keller & Papasan, 2013.

## 1. The framing (pp. 166–167)

> "Focus is a matter of deciding what things you're not going to do." — John Carmack (p. 166)

Four specific thieves steal time-blocked productivity. Each has a named defense.

The chapter opens with the **Good Samaritan Experiment** (Darley & Batson, 1973) (pp. 166, 190–191). Princeton seminary students were told they were late to deliver a sermon on the Good Samaritan. On the way, they passed a man slumped in a doorway, coughing and groaning. **Ninety percent of the rushed students failed to stop and help.** The decisive variable wasn't ethics or training — it was **time**. The thieves steal your time, which steals your values.

## 2. The four thieves — exact names, exact order (p. 167)

1. Inability to Say "No"
2. Fear of Chaos
3. Poor Health Habits
4. Environment Doesn't Support Your Goals

---

## 3. Thief 1 — Inability to Say "No" (pp. 167–173)

### The rule
> "One 'yes' must be defended over time by 1,000 'nos'." (p. 167)

> "When you say yes to something, it's imperative that you understand what you're saying no to." (p. 168)

> "The art of saying yes is, by default, the art of saying no." (p. 168)

### Case study — Steve Jobs at Apple (pp. 167–168)
After returning in 1997, Jobs cut Apple's product line from **350 to 10** over two years. "That's 340 nos." At MacWorld 1997:
> "focusing is about saying no."

### How to say no (p. 169)
**Seth Godin's rule**: with respect, promptly, with a lead to someone who might say yes. Don't ghost.

### Alternative tactics
- **Redirect** to a better "yes-er" (p. 170).
- **Leverage yeses**: FAQ docs, checklists, scripts, recorded answers so you're not re-solving the same request (p. 170).
- **"No, for now."** (p. 181) — preserves the relationship without committing.

### The Three-Foot Rule (p. 170)
> "A request must be connected to my ONE Thing for me to consider it."

### Authority quotes
- **Sidney Howard** (*Gone with the Wind* screenwriter): "One-half of knowing what you want is knowing what you must give up before you get it." (p. 168)
- **Bill Cosby**, *Ebony* 1977: "I don't know the key to success, but the key to failure is trying to please everybody." (p. 171)

### Warning story — the married mother of two (p. 171)
She agreed with her family that nothing at home would change while she pursued her goal. Her environment sabotaged her. Lesson: saying yes to ONE Thing requires renegotiating the yeses your *environment* made for you.

## 4. Thief 2 — Fear of Chaos (pp. 172–175)

### The rule
> "When you strive for greatness, chaos is guaranteed to show up." (p. 173)

**Francis Ford Coppola**: "anything you build on a large scale or with intense passion invites chaos." (p. 173)

> "Messes are inevitable when you focus on just one thing." (p. 172)

### How to defeat it
- Accept loose ends as the price of the block, not evidence you're failing.
- **Trade time with others.** They protect your block; you protect theirs.
- Enlist kids / parents / partners to cover the domains you're out of balance in during the block.
- "If you have to beg, then beg. If you have to barter, then barter... Don't sacrifice your time block on the altar of 'I just can't make it work'" (p. 174).

## 5. Thief 3 — Poor Health Habits (pp. 175–179)

### The rule
> "Personal energy mismanagement is a silent thief of productivity." (p. 175)

> "High achievement and extraordinary results require big energy." (p. 176)

### The Highly Productive Person's Daily Energy Plan (p. 177)
1. **Meditate and pray** for spiritual energy.
2. **Eat right, exercise, sleep sufficiently** for physical energy.
3. **Hug, kiss, and laugh** with loved ones for emotional energy.
4. **Set goals, plan, and calendar** for mental energy.
5. **Time block your ONE Thing** for business energy.

### Specifics (pp. 176–177)
- Nutritious breakfast.
- Plan all weekly meals at once.
- **Pedometer target: 10,000 steps/day.** If you haven't walked them by end of day, making hitting 10,000 your ONE "exercise" Thing that day.
- **8 hours of sleep.**

> "When you spend the early hours energizing yourself, you get pulled through the rest of the day with little additional effort." (p. 177)

### Why it's called a thief
Willpower depends on fuel (Ch 7, pp. 56–57). The 2007 *JPSP* nine-study willpower + nutrition research showed placebo-sweetener subjects made ~2× more errors than real-sugar subjects. Low blood sugar → low prefrontal cortex → default behavior. Missing sleep or skipping meals silently drains the battery you need for the ONE Thing block.

## 6. Thief 4 — Environment Doesn't Support Your Goals (pp. 177–182)

Environment = **People + Place** (Fig. 33, p. 179).

### People

> "No one succeeds alone and no one fails alone. Pay attention to the people around you." (p. 180)

Surround yourself with supportive, success-minded people — creates a "positive spiral of success" (p. 178).

**Christakis & Fowler (2007), *Connected* — the obesity study** (pp. 179, 203–204):
> "if one of your close friends becomes obese, you're **57 percent more likely** to do the same."

**Study in *Social Development*** (nearly 500 school-age participants, p. 180): "children who establish and maintain relationships with high-achieving students experience gains in their report card grades."

**Oprah Winfrey**: "Surround yourself only with people who are going to lift you higher." (p. 180)

**"Attitude thieves"** — negative, unfulfilled people near you. "Attitude is contagious; it spreads easily" (p. 178).

### Place

**Trial-run your daily path** (p. 181):
> "Walk through the path you'll take each day, and eradicate all the sight and sound thieves that you find."

Keller's personal environment design (p. 181):
- Check email quickly, then move on.
- Never read the newspaper.
- Keep TV closed.
- Avoid the office coffee pot and info boards (chatty-neighbor magnets).

> "When you clear the path to success — that's when you consistently get there." (p. 181)

### Lily Tomlin (p. 181)
> "The road to success is always under construction."

## 9. Anti-patterns

- **Trying to please everyone** (Cosby line, p. 171).
- **Letting chaos paralyze you** instead of trusting the ONE Thing will come through (p. 175).
- **"Cheating" on health to hit goals** — staying up late, skipping meals, ignoring exercise. Silently kills willpower (p. 176).
- **Surrounding yourself with "attitude thieves"** (p. 178).
- **Not trial-walking your daily path** — sight and sound thieves you don't see never get removed (p. 181).

## 10. Checklist — the four thieves, defended

**Say no.**
- [ ] Three-Foot Rule in place ("not connected to my ONE Thing → no").
- [ ] Default phrase drafted: "No, for now."
- [ ] FAQ / script for the 3 most-common requests.

**Accept chaos.**
- [ ] Partner / family briefed on the block and what will be chaotic.
- [ ] Reciprocal block-protection deal with one peer.
- [ ] Willing to bargain/barter for time-off coverage.

**Health.**
- [ ] 10,000-step target tracked.
- [ ] 8 hours of sleep scheduled.
- [ ] Breakfast planned. Weekly meals planned.
- [ ] Morning ritual: spiritual + physical + emotional + mental + business energy (5 categories, in order).

**Environment.**
- [ ] Audit 5 people you spend most time with — who lifts, who drains?
- [ ] Physical path to the bunker trial-walked.
- [ ] Sight thieves removed (papers, visible TV, visible phone).
- [ ] Sound thieves removed (notifications off, headphones, closed door).
