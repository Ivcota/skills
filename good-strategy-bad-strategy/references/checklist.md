# Strategy Audit Checklist

Run on any document presented as "strategy." 10-item score, pass threshold 8/10.

## The Kernel

- [ ] **1. Diagnosis present** — one sentence names the structural obstacle.
- [ ] **2. Guiding policy present** — one to two sentences describing the approach (not goals).
- [ ] **3. Coherent actions present** — 3–5 concrete actions, each reinforcing at least one other.

## The Four Hallmarks (flip — these must be ABSENT)

- [ ] **4. No fluff** — stripping buzzwords leaves non-obvious claims.
- [ ] **5. Faces the challenge** — the obstacle is explicitly named and not hidden.
- [ ] **6. Strategy ≠ goals** — the document has a plan distinct from its aspirations.
- [ ] **7. Objectives are sharp** — not a dog's-dinner, not blue-sky; each with a mechanism.

## Sources of Power

- [ ] **8. At least one source of power named** — leverage / focus / proximate / chain-link / design / dynamics / advantage / inertia / growth-from-capability.

## Hard Choices

- [ ] **9. Explicit sacrifice** — a sentence stating what you will NOT do.
- [ ] **10. Named time window** — specific reason competitors cannot respond within 12 months.

## Scoring

| Score | Interpretation | Next step |
|---|---|---|
| 10/10 | Real strategy | Ship. Schedule 6-month review. |
| 8–9/10 | Near-strategy with one fixable gap | Close the named gap; re-audit. |
| 5–7/10 | Strategy dressed as strategy | Return to Step 2 of process.md. |
| <5/10 | Not a strategy; it is a plan, a goal list, or fluff | Restart from diagnosis. |

## Common Failure Patterns (diagnostic shortcuts)

- Passes 1–3 (has a kernel) but fails 4–7 (fluff remains) → edit, don't rewrite.
- Fails 1 (no diagnosis) but passes 3 (actions listed) → almost always a dog's-dinner of initiatives masquerading as strategy. Rewrite the diagnosis first.
- Passes 1 (diagnosis) but fails 9 (no sacrifice) → leadership hasn't made the choice. Return the document with the question: "what are we giving up?"
- Passes 1–7 but fails 8 (no source of power) → strategy is honest but structurally weak; find or create asymmetry.

## Copy for the Review Meeting

- "Strip the buzzwords — what non-obvious claim remains?"
- "Point me to the sentence that names the obstacle."
- "This looks like a goal. What is the approach?"
- "These twelve 'priorities' — which two are the real ones?"
- "What are we explicitly choosing not to do?"
- "Why can't [strongest competitor] respond within 12 months?"
- "Universal buy-in — did we actually make a choice?"
