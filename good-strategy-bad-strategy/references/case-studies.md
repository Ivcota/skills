# Case Studies

Each case is mapped to Rumelt's Kernel (Diagnosis → Guiding Policy → Coherent Action) and the Source(s) of Power it illustrates. Only cases with citable support are included.

## Apple (1998, Jobs' return)

- **Diagnosis:** Product-line sprawl had destroyed focus; Apple was losing money on a dozen variants while Microsoft dominated. ([Rekhi](https://www.sachinrekhi.com/p/good-strategy-bad-strategy-richard-rumelt))
- **Guiding Policy:** Radical simplification — survive first, then design for a post-PC world.
- **Coherent Actions:** Product line cut to a 2×2 grid (consumer/pro × desktop/laptop); kill peripherals; re-focus engineering on design; restore profitability before innovating.
- **Sources of Power:** Focus (segment attack), Design (coherent product system), Leverage (minimum resources where they matter most). ([Rekhi](https://www.sachinrekhi.com/p/good-strategy-bad-strategy-richard-rumelt))

## Nvidia (1990s–2000s)

- **Diagnosis:** 3D graphics cards were the future; the prevailing multimedia-chip bet was on the wrong side of the wave. ([Penguin](https://penguinrandomhousehighereducation.com/book/?isbn=9780307886231))
- **Guiding Policy:** Abandon the multimedia approach; focus entirely on superior GPUs for PCs.
- **Coherent Actions:** Three parallel design teams; ~6-month product cadence; tight alignment with DirectX; relentless iteration on GPU performance.
- **Sources of Power:** Focus + Dynamics (riding the 3D wave) + Leverage (staggered cadence outpaced competitors). ([Penguin](https://penguinrandomhousehighereducation.com/book/?isbn=9780307886231))

## Wal-Mart (Sam Walton era)

- **Diagnosis:** Small towns were underserved by existing big-box retail; the prevailing wisdom held stores couldn't prosper in markets under 100k population.
- **Guiding Policy:** Make the **network** of stores the unit of competition, not the single store.
- **Coherent Actions:** Cluster stores around regional distribution centers; invest in logistics technology; standardize operations; concentrate geographically before expanding.
- **Sources of Power:** Design (chain-link system of store + DC + logistics), Growth (result of distinctive capability, not goal), Advantage (cost leadership from density), Inertia (Kmart's retail-footprint commitments).
- Rumelt: "Kmart saw Wal-Mart like Goliath saw David — smaller and less experienced in the big leagues. The network replaced the store." Walton "broke the old definition of a store." ([Blas](https://blas.com/good-strategy-bad-strategy/))

## Desert Storm (Schwarzkopf's "left hook", 1991)

- **Diagnosis:** A frontal assault on entrenched Iraqi positions would be costly; Iraqi attention could be misdirected.
- **Guiding Policy:** Misdirect Iraqi command, then flank with concentrated armor through the weakest sector — a classic envelopment.
- **Coherent Actions:** Visible coastal feint; covert repositioning of VII and XVIII Corps west; concentrated armored breakthrough on the left.
- **Sources of Power:** Leverage (concentration against the weakest flank), Proximate Objectives (bounded 100-hour ground campaign). ([WebSearch result summarizing Rumelt](https://www.amazon.com/Good-Strategy-Bad-Difference-Matters/dp/0307886239))
- Rumelt notes the strategy was "foreshadowed in the US Army's own field manual titled 'Operations'" — the surprise was that "such a pure and focused strategy was actually implemented." ([WebSearch](https://juststartwith.com/en/richard-rumelt/good-strategy-bad-strategy/))

## International Harvester (1979) — Bad Strategy Illustration

- **Diagnosis (missing):** The plan projected ag-equipment market share 16% → 20% without naming Harvester's inefficient production facilities or worst-in-industry labor relations. ([McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/the-perils-of-bad-strategy))
- **Guiding Policy (missing):** None — only a bundle of divisional share-growth targets.
- **Coherent Actions (missing):** Initiatives per division, uncoordinated across the company.
- **Outcome:** A six-month strike hit; the company was dismembered within a decade. The plan had no capacity to absorb the obstacle it had refused to name.
- **Lesson:** A strategy that refuses to face the challenge fails the moment the challenge arrives.

## Starbucks (Schultz era)

- **Diagnosis:** Mass-market coffee was commodified; consumers would pay premium for an experience.
- **Guiding Policy:** Vertically integrate the coffee experience — from bean sourcing through in-store atmosphere.
- **Coherent Actions:** Control roasting; standardize in-store design; train baristas; own prime retail locations.
- **Source of Power:** Design — "vertical integration enabling mutual adjustment of multiple elements." ([Blas](https://blas.com/good-strategy-bad-strategy/))

## IKEA (chain-link illustration)

- **Diagnosis:** Furniture retail was high-cost, slow-delivery, and space-inefficient.
- **Guiding Policy:** Flat-pack, self-service, destination-store model — every link optimized for low cost and self-assembly.
- **Coherent Actions:** In-house design optimized for flat-pack; logistics and inventory built around the flat-pack format; destination store with restaurant and warehouse pickup; low-cost assembly design.
- **Source of Power:** Chain-Link Systems — "performance limited by the weakest link." Competitors cannot copy one piece in isolation. ([Zych](https://jlzych.com/2018/06/27/notes-from-good-strategy-bad-strategy/))

## Netflix vs. Blockbuster (inertia illustration)

- **Diagnosis:** Blockbuster's 9,000+ retail stores were a fixed-cost commitment that locked them into the physical model.
- **Guiding Policy (Netflix):** Exploit the competitor's structural inability to abandon its footprint.
- **Coherent Actions (Netflix):** Mail-DVD → streaming → originals; each move widened the gap.
- **Source of Power:** Inertia — competitor's predictable non-response. ([Zych](https://jlzych.com/2018/06/27/notes-from-good-strategy-bad-strategy/))

## 7-Eleven Japan (Rekhi summary of Rumelt)

- **Diagnosis:** Global convenience-store model was ineffective in Japan; local dynamics required adaptation. ([Rekhi](https://www.sachinrekhi.com/p/good-strategy-bad-strategy-richard-rumelt))
- **Guiding Policy:** Offer broad selection with rapid new-product cycles tuned to local taste.
- **Coherent Actions:** Local insight-collection system; partnerships with second/third-tier manufacturers to enable quick private-label offerings.
- **Source of Power:** Dynamics (ride local taste cycles) + Chain-Link (insight-to-shelf pipeline).

## Pattern Across Cases

Real strategies name the challenge, pick an approach, concentrate resources, and exploit a specific asymmetry competitors cannot neutralize quickly. Bad strategies skip the diagnosis, substitute goals, and pursue size as if it were a source of power.
