# Zero to One — Worksheet & Checklist

The *doing* document. Run through this with a real plan in hand (yours or one you're diagnosing). Every item maps back to a framework section. Decision rules use Thiel's own thresholds.

---

## 1. The Zero-to-One Worksheet (12 Steps)

### Step 1 — Contrarian question [Ch. 1 / §1]
- [ ] Answer: "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"
- [ ] Does the answer take the form "Most people believe X, but the truth is the opposite of X"?
- [ ] Does it describe the future, not restate the present?
- [ ] Is it *unpopular* in your peer group? (courage test — "courage is in even shorter supply than genius")
- [ ] Business version answered: "What valuable company is nobody building?"

### Step 2 — Secret [Ch. 8 / §6]
- [ ] Named the secret in one sentence
- [ ] Classified: secret of **nature**, secret about **people**, or both
- [ ] Is anyone else looking here? (If yes — go narrower)
- [ ] Would the field show up in a Harvard course catalog? (If yes — too standardized)
- [ ] Have I shared it only with conspirators, not broadcast it?

### Step 3 — Beachhead [Ch. 5 / §3]
- [ ] Named a small group of particular people, concentrated together, served by few or no competitors
- [ ] Can I get a **big share** of this small market in ≤18 months?
- [ ] Avoided the "1% of a $100B market" framing?
- [ ] Beachhead is **real** (not a rhetorical narrowing)?
- [ ] Named ≥1 adjacent market I'll sequence into after dominance

### Step 4 — Durability (4 Characteristics) [Ch. 5 / §3]
- [ ] Proprietary tech is ≥10x better than nearest substitute on ≥1 important dimension
- [ ] Network effects are valuable to the *first few* users (not only at scale)
- [ ] Economies of scale built into day-one design (software-like fixed costs)
- [ ] Brand rests on underlying substance, not polish
- [ ] Business will still throw off cash 10–20 years from now

### Step 5 — Definite optimistic plan [Ch. 6 / §4]
- [ ] Written a concrete 5–10 year vision (not "iterate and see")
- [ ] Picked the *one best thing* to do — striving to be a "monopoly of one"
- [ ] Plan is bold; "a bad plan is better than no plan"
- [ ] No portfolio/optionality hedging

### Step 6 — Power-law concentration [Ch. 7 / §5]
- [ ] Identified the one market that dominates all others
- [ ] Identified the one distribution channel that dominates all others
- [ ] Over-invested in the one decision that will fall furthest right on the curve
- [ ] Refused kitchen-sink diversification

### Step 7 — Foundations [Ch. 9 / §7]
- [ ] Co-founder has shared prehistory with me (not "rolling dice")
- [ ] Ownership / Possession / Control identified and aligned
- [ ] Board of 3 (max 5 — publicly held exempted)
- [ ] Everyone full-time (no consultants, part-timers, remote-only core)
- [ ] CEO cash pay ≤ $150K/yr
- [ ] Equity with vesting; cap table kept private
- [ ] Foundation set *before* launch — "beginnings are special"

### Step 8 — Mafia culture [Ch. 10 / §7]
- [ ] From the outside, team is "different in the same way"
- [ ] Each team member owns exactly **one** thing
- [ ] Recruiting is done in-house (not outsourced)
- [ ] No perk war — culture rests on mission, not amenities
- [ ] Fanatically *right* about a real secret (not cult-level *wrong*)

### Step 9 — Distribution motion [Ch. 11 / §8]
- [ ] Picked one channel that matches deal size (see picker below)
- [ ] CAC < CLV proven or plausible
- [ ] Committed — not hedging across 5 channels
- [ ] Named the human doing the selling (if I don't see one — it's me)

### Step 10 — Man + machine [Ch. 12 / §9]
- [ ] Product *empowers* humans — does not try to replace them
- [ ] Asked: "How can computers help humans solve hard problems?"
- [ ] Rejected "replace the analyst" framings; embraced hybrid

### Step 11 — Seven Questions pass [Ch. 13 / §9]
- [ ] Scored the plan on all seven (see scorecard)
- [ ] Score is ≥5/7 — if lower, fix the zeros before betting

### Step 12 — Founder's paradox [Ch. 14]
- [ ] Founder is making authoritative, decades-long decisions
- [ ] Founder has not been swapped for a "professional CEO"
- [ ] Founder isn't believing his own myth (self-check quarterly)

## 2. The Seven-Questions Scorecard

| # | Question | Answer | Score 0–1 |
|---|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | **Engineering** — breakthrough, 10x better, not incremental? | | |
| 2 | **Timing** — why now, what inflection? | | |
| 3 | **Monopoly** — big share of a small market? | | |
| 4 | **People** — right team, no suits? | | |
| 5 | **Distribution** — specific, owned channel, CAC < CLV? | | |
| 6 | **Durability** — defensible in 10–20 years? | | |
| 7 | **Secret** — unique opportunity others don't see? | | |
|   | **Total** | | **/7** |

**Decision rule:**
- **7/7** → ship (Tesla-level)
- **5–6/7** → might work; fix the zeros first
- **<5/7** → "hoping for a miracle" — do not bet the company

- [ ] Co-founder has shared prehistory with me

- [ ] Board of 3 (max 5)

## 4. The Contrarian-Answer Grading Rubric

| Answer type | Example | Grade |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Popular truth | "Education is broken" | **F** |
| Side in a familiar debate | "There is no God" / "America is exceptional" | **F** |
| Non-consensus but about the present | "Most SaaS pricing is miscalibrated today" | **C** |
| Non-consensus about the future | "The next decade of X won't look like the last" | **A** |
| Unpopular in your own peer group AND about the future | (the one you're afraid to say out loud) | **A+** |

Pass threshold: **A or A+**. Anything lower is not a 0→1 thesis.

## 5. The Distribution-Channel Picker (by deal size)

| Avg deal size | Dominant channel | Example |
|---------------|------------------|---------|
| **$1M+** | Complex sales (founder-led, years-long, no separate salesforce) | SpaceX, Palantir |
| **$10K–$100K** | Personal sales (dedicated sales team, ladder up through org) | Box |
| **$1K–$10K** | **Distribution doldrums — avoid** (too cheap for personal sales, too expensive for ads) | — |
| **<$1K consumer subscription** | Marketing / advertising | Consumer playbook |
| **Viral use-case** | Paid referral loops, short cycle time | PayPal ($20 CAC, 7% daily growth) |

**Decision rule:** pick *one* and commit. "If you can get just one distribution channel to work, you have a great business. If you try for several but don't nail one, you're finished."

## 6. Monopoly-Characteristic Diagnostic

- [ ] Proprietary tech **≥10x** better than nearest substitute in ≥1 important dimension?
- [ ] Network effects already valuable at very small scale (first few users)?
- [ ] Economies of scale built into the design from day one (software-like fixed costs)?
- [ ] Brand resting on real substance (not just first/loudest)?

Rule: not a checklist to tick — a lens. But if you can't honestly tick **one** with substance, you don't have durability yet.

## 7. Red Flags in a Pitch / Memo

| Red flag (spotted in the deck) | Fix |
|--------------------------------|-----|
| "We'll capture 1% of a $100B market" | Pick a concentrated beachhead and take a big share of a small market |
| "We're the Uber of X" / framed by opposition to an incumbent | Rewrite around your secret, not the rival — "it can't be completely new" if defined by opposition |
| "Stay lean and flexible" as the whole plan | Write a concrete 5–10 year plan — iteration alone can't take you 0→1 |
| Tech CEO wearing a suit to the pitch | Hard pass — "never invest in a tech CEO that wears a suit" |
| Distribution is a single line on slide 14 | Pair product with a specific owned channel matched to deal size — sales is ≥ product |
| Five distribution channels listed, none dominant | Kill four; nail one — power law |
| "We're hiring top consultants / part-timers for launch" | Full-time only — on the bus or off the bus |
| Board of 7+ advisors and investors | Cut to 3 (max 5) — big boards don't govern |
| Founder CEO salary >$150K cash | Sub-$150K + equity; cash pay over $150K "defends status quo" |
| "Big data + AI will replace the [analyst / doctor / recruiter]" | Reframe as complement — "how can computers help humans solve hard problems?" |
