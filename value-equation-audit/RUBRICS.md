# Scoring Rubrics

Rate each lever 1-10 using these bands. Cite the evidence from the intake that justifies the score.

## Lever 1: Dream Outcome

The ideal end state the customer wants. Bigger, more specific, more emotional = higher score.

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-2 | Vague, generic, low emotional charge | "Improve your marketing" |
| 3-4 | Somewhat specific, moderate desire | "Get more leads for your business" |
| 5-6 | Specific and desirable, but common | "Generate 50 qualified leads per month" |
| 7-8 | Highly specific, emotionally charged, quantified | "Add $100K in annual revenue within 6 months" |
| 9-10 | Life-changing, deeply emotional, uniquely articulated | "Replace your 9-to-5 income in 120 days and never miss another school pickup" |

## Lever 2: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement

How confident the prospect is that *they specifically* will get the result. Proof, guarantees, named system = higher score.

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-2 | No proof, no system, no guarantee | "Trust me, this works" |
| 3-4 | A few testimonials or a vague process | "Our clients love us" with 2 quotes |
| 5-6 | Solid social proof, clear methodology | 20+ testimonials, a defined framework |
| 7-8 | Extensive proof, named system, guarantee | Named methodology, case studies, money-back guarantee |
| 9-10 | Overwhelming proof, performance guarantee, track record | 500+ success stories, performance-based guarantee, public metrics |

## Lever 3: Time Delay (higher score = faster)

Gap between purchase and result. Score is inverted so 10 = immediate. When computing composite, use `(11 - score)` as the denominator weight.

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-2 | Results take 6+ months with no quick wins | "Results vary; most see changes after a year" |
| 3-4 | Results in 3-6 months, minor quick wins | "You'll start seeing traction in a few months" |
| 5-6 | Results in 1-3 months, clear milestones | "First results in 30 days, full results in 90" |
| 7-8 | Results in 2-4 weeks, strong Day 1 win | "Set up in 1 hour, first leads in 7 days" |
| 9-10 | Immediate or same-day results | "Your first campaign goes live today" |

## Lever 4: Effort & Sacrifice (higher score = easier)

Everything the customer must do, learn, endure, or give up. Score is inverted so 10 = zero effort. When computing composite, use `(11 - score)` as the denominator weight.

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-2 | High effort, steep learning curve, major sacrifice | "Complete this 200-hour certification, then implement on your own" |
| 3-4 | Significant effort, some hand-holding | "Follow these 40 lessons and build your own system" |
| 5-6 | Moderate effort, clear instructions, some automation | "Follow the 10-step playbook with templates provided" |
| 7-8 | Low effort, mostly done-for-you, minimal decisions | "We build it, you approve it. 3 decisions total." |
| 9-10 | Zero effort, fully done-for-you, no sacrifice | "We handle everything. You just collect the results." |

## Composite Score

**Formula:** `(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / ((11 - Time Delay) × (11 - Effort))`

Perfect offer: (10 × 10) / (1 × 1) = **100**
Poor offer: (2 × 2) / (9 × 9) = **0.05**

### Interpretation bands

| Composite | Band | Meaning |
|-----------|------|---------|
| 50-100 | **Grand Slam** | Premium pricing justified. Focus on distribution, not the offer. |
| 20-49 | **Strong** | Good offer. One or two levers worth optimizing. |
| 5-19 | **Average** | Multiple levers need work. Likely competing on price. |
| Below 5 | **Weak** | Fundamental redesign needed before more marketing spend. |

## Diagnosis rules

- The **weakest lever** has the largest gap to 10.
- If two levers tie in gap-to-10, prefer optimizing a **denominator lever** (Time Delay or Effort) — denominators have larger impact on the composite.
- If Perceived Likelihood is below 5 and the business is pre-launch with no customers, call this out explicitly: no amount of copy will fix a trust gap; the first move is getting 3-5 real case studies.
- If Dream Outcome is below 5, no other lever matters — fix the "what" before fixing the "how fast" or "how easy".
