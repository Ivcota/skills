# Persona → Product — Problem-First Product Ideation

Take a buyer profile and surface product or service ideas that the persona would actually buy — grounded in their real problems, transformation desires, and psychological constraints.

References: [Evaluation Rubric](evaluation-rubric.md)

## Input

Accepts one of:
- A buyer profile generated by the `buyer-profile` skill (just say "use the profile above")
- A pasted buyer profile
- A brief persona description (will generate a quick profile first before ideating)

## Process

### Phase 1: Extract Problem Signals

Pull these five signals from the profile. If any are missing, ask the user before continuing.

- [ ] **Core problem** — the central, urgent issue
- [ ] **Failed solutions** — what they've already tried and rejected
- [ ] **Won't-dos** — sacrifices, risks, or tasks they refuse
- [ ] **Transformation desires** — the Magic Genie outcomes they want
- [ ] **Top objections** — reasons they'd hesitate to buy

### Phase 2: Ideate 5–7 Concepts

Generate product and service concepts that:
1. Directly address the core problem
2. Do NOT replicate a failed solution (or explain why this version is different)
3. Require nothing on the won't-do list
4. Deliver at least one transformation desire
5. Cover a mix of formats: SaaS, physical product, service, community, course, tool, done-for-you, etc.

### Phase 3: Evaluate Each Concept

Score each concept against the persona using the rubric in [Evaluation Rubric](evaluation-rubric.md). Surface:
- Strongest emotional hook (which fear or desire drives purchase)
- Primary objection risk (which objection could kill the sale)
- Constraint risk (does it accidentally require a won't-do?)

### Phase 4: Present & Rank

Output a ranked list. Lead with the concepts with the highest desire alignment and lowest objection risk.

## Output Format

For each concept:

```
## [Concept Name]
**Format:** [SaaS / service / product / community / course / etc.]
**One-line pitch:** [Written in the persona's voice]
**Problem solved:** [Tied to a specific profile element]
**Why they buy:** [Specific emotional driver from the profile]
**Why they won't resist:** [Addresses a won't-do or objection]
**Objection risk:** [The one objection most likely to kill this]
```

After all concepts, add a **Which to build first** section: 2–3 sentences recommending the highest-fit concept and why, grounded in the persona's psychology.

## Principles

- **Constraints are features** — the won't-do list is a product spec. Build around it, not against it.
- **Emotion closes deals** — every concept must have a clear emotional hook, not just a functional one.
- **Specificity over range** — a product that perfectly solves one problem beats one that partially solves five.
- **Failed solutions are market research** — if the persona already tried X, your version needs a named reason to be different.
