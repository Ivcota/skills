# Evaluation Rubric

Score each product/service concept against the persona on three axes. Use 1–5 for each.

---

## Axis 1: Desire Alignment (1–5)

Does this concept deliver on the persona's transformation desires?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Directly delivers ≥2 Magic Genie outcomes |
| 4 | Delivers 1 Magic Genie outcome clearly |
| 3 | Implies transformation but doesn't name it |
| 2 | Adjacent to what they want but not the core fantasy |
| 1 | Misses the transformation entirely |

## Axis 2: Friction Score (1–5, lower = less friction)

Does this concept require anything on the won't-do list?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Requires nothing they've refused; maps to their existing behavior |
| 2 | Requires minimal effort they haven't explicitly rejected |
| 3 | Requires one borderline won't-do (explain the difference) |
| 4 | Requires something they've sworn off — needs strong reframe |
| 5 | Directly violates a won't-do or repeats a failed solution |

## Axis 3: Objection Exposure (1–5, lower = less exposed)

How many of the persona's top objections does this concept trigger?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Triggers 0–1 objections |
| 2 | Triggers 1–2 but they're easily addressed |
| 3 | Triggers 2–3; needs explicit handling in positioning |
| 4 | Triggers 3–4; requires significant trust-building |
| 5 | Triggers all major objections — high-risk concept |

---

## Composite Ranking

**Final score** = Desire Alignment − Friction Score − Objection Exposure

Higher is better. Concepts with scores ≥ 5 are strong candidates. Scores below 2 need significant repositioning or should be cut.

---

## Tie-breaker: Differentiation from Failed Solutions

If two concepts score equally, prefer the one that is most clearly differentiated from the persona's failed past solutions. A concept that echoes something they've already tried will face a much harder sale, regardless of how good it is.
