# Warm Outreach: Your Fastest Path to First Leads

Warm outreach is contacting people who already know you -- friends, family, followers, past customers, contacts, anyone in your existing network. These people already trust you, which means you skip the hardest part of lead generation: building trust from zero. Warm outreach is free, immediate, and the single best way to start any business or launch any new offer.

## Who Counts as "Warm"

Anyone who has given you permission to contact them:
- Friends and family
- Social media followers and connections
- Current customers
- Previous customers
- Email subscribers
- Former colleagues
- Community members (church, gym, school, clubs)
- Anyone who has previously responded to a message from you

**A warm audience is defined by trust, not closeness.** A LinkedIn connection you've exchanged 3 messages with is warm. A stranger who follows your Instagram because they liked one post is warm. Permission + some level of recognition = warm.

## The ACA Framework

The ACA framework (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask) is the conversational structure for warm outreach. It ensures your messages feel natural, not salesy.

### A -- Acknowledge

Restate what they said (or what you know about their situation) in your own words. This demonstrates active listening and shows you care about them, not just about making a sale.

**Examples:**
- "I saw you just launched your new website -- that's a huge milestone."
- "You mentioned you were hiring a marketing manager. How's that search going?"
- "I noticed you've been posting a lot about [topic]. Sounds like it's been on your mind."

### C -- Compliment

Compliment them on what they shared, ideally linking it to a positive character trait. This raises their status and builds rapport.

**Examples:**
- "That takes real guts to put yourself out there like that. Most people just talk about it."
- "You've always been one of the most connected people I know."
- "The fact that you're even thinking about this puts you ahead of 90% of people."

### A -- Ask

Transition naturally to what you're offering. This can be a direct ask or an indirect ask (for a referral).

**Direct ask examples:**
- "I'm actually working on something that helps [avatars] with exactly that. Would you be open to hearing about it?"
- "I just launched [product/service]. I think it might be right up your alley. Can I share the details?"

**Indirect (referral) ask examples:**
- "I'm helping [avatars] solve [problem]. Do you know anyone who might be dealing with that?"
- "I'm looking for 5 [avatars] to test my new [product/service]. Know anyone who'd be a fit?"

## The 10-Step Warm Outreach Process

### Step 1: Get a List
Scrape all your contacts across every platform. Export contacts from your phone, email, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter. Compile into one master list. Aim for 1,000+ contacts.

**Where to find your contacts:**
- Phone contacts
- Email contacts (Gmail, Outlook)
- LinkedIn connections
- Facebook friends
- Instagram followers/following
- Twitter/X followers
- WhatsApp/Telegram groups
- Alumni networks
- Professional associations
- Old customer databases

### Step 2: Pick the Platform
Choose the platform where you have the most contacts and where messaging feels natural. Usually LinkedIn for B2B, Instagram/Facebook for B2C, or text/phone for local.

### Step 3: Send Personalized Messages
Use something specific you know about the contact as the reason for reaching out. Never mass-message.

**Good:** "Hey Sarah, saw you just got promoted to VP of Marketing at Acme. Congrats! How are you settling in?"

**Bad:** "Hey! I have a new product that I think you'd love. Check it out at [link]."

### Step 4: Reach Out to 100 Per Day
Apply the Rule of 100. Contact 100 people per day. Each contact should be attempted up to 3 times (once per day for 3 days, or once per week for physical mail) until you get a response.

### Step 5: Warm Them Up
Don't pitch immediately. Have a genuine conversation first. Ask questions. Listen. Show interest in their life. The ACA framework guides this.

### Step 6: Ask for Referrals
Even if they're not a fit, ask: "Do you know anyone who might be dealing with [problem]?" This multiplies your reach because one warm contact can introduce you to several new leads.

### Step 7: Make an Easy Offer
Give it away FREE initially. The goal of the first 5 clients is testimonials and referrals, not revenue. Free removes all friction from the decision.

### Step 8: Work the List Again
Go back to the top and work remaining contacts. People who didn't respond the first time may respond on the second or third attempt (life is busy).

### Step 9: Reduce Your Discount
After every 5 new customers, reduce the discount:
- First 5: Free
- Next 5: 80% off
- Next 5: 60% off
- Next 5: 40% off
- Next 5: 20% off
- Normal price

This gives you a steady stream of customers while increasing revenue progressively.

### Step 10: Keep Your List Warm
Regularly provide value to your warm audience through content, check-ins, and occasional outreach. A warm list that goes cold is a missed opportunity. Stay top of mind.

## Reactivation Campaigns

### The 9-Word Email (Dean Jackson)

For re-engaging past customers or dead leads, send an extremely short, personal email:

**Subject:** [First name]?
**Body:** "Are you still looking for help with [specific thing they originally inquired about]?"

That's it. No links, no branding, no pitch. Just a genuine question. This format gets responses because it looks like a real person wrote it (because a real person did).

### Past Customer Reactivation

For customers who haven't purchased in 6+ months:

1. "Hey [Name], it's been a while! How's [thing they bought from you] working out?"
2. Listen and respond naturally
3. If positive: "That's great to hear. We actually just launched [new thing]. Thought of you because [reason]."
4. If negative: "Sorry to hear that. What happened? I'd love to make it right."

### Event-Based Reactivation

Use life events and business milestones as natural reasons to reconnect:
- New job/promotion: "Congrats on the new role! How's the transition going?"
- Company funding: "Saw you raised a round. Exciting times. How are you planning to invest it?"
- Birthday/anniversary: "Happy birthday! Hope it's a great year for you."
- Industry event: "Are you going to [conference]? Would love to catch up there."

## Referral Ask Templates

### The Direct Ask (after delivering value)
"Hey [Name], I'm so glad [positive result] worked out. Quick question -- do you know 1-2 other [avatars] who might be dealing with [problem]? I'd love to help them the same way."

### The Connector Ask (for well-connected contacts)
"You're one of the most connected people I know in [industry]. I'm looking for [specific avatar] who's struggling with [specific problem]. Anyone come to mind?"

### The New Launch Ask
"I'm launching [new offer] specifically for [avatars]. It helps them [dream outcome] in [timeframe]. I'm looking for my first 10 clients. Know anyone who might be interested?"

### The Reverse Referral
Give first, then ask: "Hey [Name], I met someone at [event] who'd be perfect for your [business]. Let me connect you two. Also -- if you know any [avatars] who need help with [problem], I'd appreciate the introduction."

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: The Master List
Export contacts from every platform. Compile into one list. Count them. If you have fewer than 500, identify 3 ways to grow your warm audience (join communities, attend events, connect on LinkedIn).

### Exercise 2: The ACA Practice Run
Write 5 ACA messages to 5 different contacts -- real messages you will actually send. Have a friend review them for authenticity (do they sound like you, or like a robot?).

### Exercise 3: The 5 Free Clients Challenge
Offer your product or service free to 5 people in your warm network. The only payment: a testimonial and 2 referrals. Track the results.

### Exercise 4: The Reactivation Sprint
Identify 50 past customers or dead leads. Send the 9-word email to all 50 over 3 days. Track response rate and conversions.
