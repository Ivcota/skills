# Lead Magnets: The Bridge Between Stranger and Customer

A lead magnet is "a complete solution to a narrow problem." It is the thing you offer for free that compels a stranger to give you their contact information and attention. A great lead magnet does double duty: it generates the lead AND warms them up for your core offer by demonstrating your expertise and revealing a larger need your paid offer solves.

## The Lead Magnet Matrix

There are three types of lead magnets (by problem type) and four delivery vehicles. Combined, they create up to 12 possible lead magnets for a single narrow problem.

### Three Types (By Problem)

#### Type 1: Problem Revealers

Make people aware of a problem they didn't know they had. Works best when the problem gets worse the longer you wait.

**How it works:** You reveal a gap between where they are and where they should be. The discovery of the gap creates urgency to close it -- and your core offer is the bridge.

**Examples:**
| Industry | Problem Revealer | What It Reveals |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| SaaS | Website speed test | "Your site loads in 8 seconds. You're losing 40% of visitors." |
| Fitness | Posture assessment | "You have 3 imbalances that will cause back pain within 6 months." |
| Marketing | Ad account audit | "You're wasting $3,000/month on 4 underperforming campaigns." |
| Real estate | Home value estimator | "Your home is worth $50K more than your current assessment." |
| Cybersecurity | Security vulnerability scan | "We found 7 critical vulnerabilities in your infrastructure." |

#### Type 2: Samples and Trials

Solve a recurring problem for a limited time. Consumables by nature have limited uses and require recurring purchases, making samples the natural lead magnet.

**How it works:** The prospect experiences the value of your product/service firsthand. When the sample runs out or the trial ends, they want more.

**Examples:**
| Industry | Sample/Trial | What Happens After |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| SaaS | 14-day free trial | They need the full product to continue working |
| Skincare | 7-day sample kit | They see results and want the full-size products |
| Coaching | Free 30-minute strategy call | They see what's possible with ongoing coaching |
| Software | Free tier with usage limits | They outgrow the free tier and upgrade |
| Food/Beverage | Free tasting or first order free | They develop a preference and reorder |

#### Type 3: One Step of a Multi-Step Process

Give away one valuable step for free. The rest come with your core offer. Works when the core offer solves a complex, multi-step problem.

**How it works:** Completing one step makes the prospect realize (1) you know what you're doing and (2) they need the other steps to get the full result.

**Examples:**
| Industry | Free Step | Remaining Steps (Paid) |
|----------|----------|----------------------|
| Marketing | "Step 1: Audit your current funnel" | Steps 2-5: Build, test, optimize, scale |
| Fitness | "Step 1: The 7-day nutrition reset" | Steps 2-4: Training program, coaching, accountability |
| Business | "Step 1: Find your niche" | Steps 2-6: Build offer, get leads, close sales, scale |
| Design | "Step 1: Brand audit worksheet" | Steps 2-4: Brand strategy, visual identity, brand guidelines |
| Finance | "Step 1: Cash flow diagnostic" | Steps 2-5: Budget, investment plan, tax optimization, monitoring |

### Four Delivery Vehicles

#### Software
Give them a tool -- spreadsheets, calculators, small software tools that do a job for them.

**Strengths:** High perceived value, immediately useful, feels premium.
**Examples:** ROI calculator, grading tool, assessment quiz, budget spreadsheet, template generator.

#### Information
Teach them something -- courses, lessons, interviews, guides, keynotes, mistakes and pitfalls, hacks and tips.

**Strengths:** Positions you as expert, scalable, easy to create.
**Examples:** Mini-course, PDF guide, cheat sheet, checklist, video tutorial, interview series.

#### Services
Be available at more times in more ways -- via video call, phone call, or in person.

**Strengths:** Highest engagement, strongest relationship building, highest conversion.
**Examples:** Free consultation, free audit call, free strategy session, free coaching call.

#### Physical Products
Give them something they can hold.

**Strengths:** Tangible, memorable, novelty factor.
**Examples:** Sample products, printed guides, branded tools, starter kits (usually "free + shipping").

### The Matrix

| | **Software** | **Information** | **Services** | **Physical** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem Revealer** | Speed test tool, vulnerability scanner | Audit guide, diagnostic checklist | Free audit call, assessment session | Printed diagnostic report |
| **Sample/Trial** | Free tier, limited feature access | First module free, preview content | Free consultation, trial session | Product sample, starter kit |
| **One Step** | Calculator for step 1, worksheet tool | Guide for step 1, video tutorial | Done-for-you step 1 | Printed workbook for step 1 |

## The Seven-Step Creation Process

### Step 1: Pick a Narrow Problem to Solve

The problem must be:
- Narrow enough to solve completely in the lead magnet
- Painful enough that people will exchange their contact info for the solution
- Connected to a larger problem your core offer solves

**Test:** Can you describe the problem in one sentence? Can the lead magnet solve it completely? Does solving it reveal a bigger problem?

### Step 2: Figure Out How to Solve It

Choose one of the three types: Problem Revealer, Sample/Trial, or One Step. The type depends on your business model and what naturally leads to your core offer.

### Step 3: Choose the Delivery Method

Match the delivery vehicle to your audience and resources:
- Software if you can build it (highest perceived value)
- Information if you have expertise (easiest to create)
- Services if you have time (highest conversion)
- Physical if you have inventory (most memorable)

### Step 4: Test the Name

The name of your lead magnet determines whether people engage. Per David Ogilvy: "When you have written your headline, you have spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar."

**Naming formulas:**
- "The [Adjective] [Asset]: [Specific Result]"
- "[Number] [Things] to [Result] in [Timeframe]"
- "The [Avatar]'s Guide to [Result]"
- "How to [Result] Without [Pain Point]"
- "Free [Tool/Template]: [What It Does]"

**Test your name:** Run a poll, A/B test landing pages, or ask 10 people in your target audience which name they'd click on.

### Step 5: Make It Easy to Consume

- Short > long (solve the problem quickly)
- Visual > text-heavy
- Actionable > theoretical
- Immediate access > delayed delivery

### Step 6: Make It Exceptionally Valuable

If you would be embarrassed to charge for it, don't give it away. Your lead magnet should be so good that people feel guilty getting it for free. This sets the expectation that your paid offer is even better.

### Step 7: Include a Clear CTA

The lead magnet must bridge to your core offer. After consuming it, the prospect should understand:
1. What their bigger problem is (revealed by solving the narrow one)
2. That you can solve it (demonstrated by the quality of the lead magnet)
3. What to do next (clear CTA to your core offer)

## Offer-Lead Magnet Alignment

The lead magnet must be a stepping stone to the core offer, not a disconnected freebie.

**Good alignment:**
| Lead Magnet | Core Offer | Connection |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Free website audit (reveals SEO problems) | SEO services ($5K/month) | Audit reveals problems → services fix them |
| Free budgeting spreadsheet | Financial planning coaching ($3K) | Spreadsheet reveals financial gaps → coaching fills them |
| Free 5-day email course on copywriting basics | Full copywriting program ($997) | Course teaches basics → program teaches advanced + implementation |
| Free trial of project management tool | Paid subscription ($99/month) | Trial hooks them → paid unlocks full power |

**Bad alignment:**
| Lead Magnet | Core Offer | Why It Fails |
|------------|-----------|-------------|
| Free meditation guide | Marketing consulting | No connection between the two |
| Free t-shirt | SaaS product | Attracts freebie seekers, not software buyers |
| Generic industry report | Done-for-you services | Report doesn't reveal a need for the services |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: The Matrix Fill
For your business, fill in the 3x4 Lead Magnet Matrix with specific ideas. You should have up to 12 possible lead magnets. Pick the top 3.

### Exercise 2: The Name Test
Write 5 names for your top lead magnet idea. Ask 10 people in your target audience to rank them. Use the winner.

### Exercise 3: The Alignment Check
Draw a line from your lead magnet to your core offer. Can you clearly explain how solving the narrow problem in the lead magnet naturally leads to the bigger problem your core offer solves? If not, choose a different narrow problem.

### Exercise 4: The Value Test
Show your lead magnet to 5 people in your target market. Ask: "Would you pay $50 for this?" If not, it's not valuable enough. Improve until the answer is yes.
