# Lead Getters: Getting Others to Advertise for You

There is a ceiling on how much one person can do. You can only send so many DMs, create so many videos, write so many emails. Lead Getters break through this ceiling by multiplying your advertising capacity. They are people or entities you recruit to execute the Core Four on your behalf. When Lead Getters do the Core Four, you get the "Core Four on Steroids" -- multiplicative scale that turns a solo effort into an army.

## The Four Types of Lead Getters

### 1. Customer Referrals

**What they are:** Existing customers who bring new leads through word-of-mouth, introductions, and recommendations.

**Why they're the most powerful:** Customer referrals are pre-qualified, pre-trusted, and pre-sold. A referred lead comes in with higher trust, higher close rate, and higher lifetime value than any other lead source. Hormozi's ad platform shut down and they didn't notice for two weeks because referrals alone were generating $500,000/week in sales.

**The two barriers to referrals:**
1. Your product is not good enough (people only refer things they genuinely love)
2. You don't directly ask (most customers would refer but no one asks them)

**The two solutions:**
1. **Make it easy** -- Create a referral mechanism that is simple and frictionless
2. **Give them a reason** -- Add incentives (financial or non-financial)

**Referral system design:**

| Mechanism | How It Works | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct ask** | Ask every customer at peak satisfaction | "Who do you know that could benefit from this?" |
| **Referral program** | Structured incentive program | "Refer a friend, get $50 credit" |
| **Give-to-give** | Give customers something to share | "Here are 3 guest passes for your friends" |
| **Results celebration** | Celebrate milestones, ask for introductions | "Congrats on [result]! Know anyone else who wants this?" |
| **Automated prompt** | Trigger ask at specific moments | Post-purchase email: "Know someone who'd love this?" |
| **Surprise referral bonus** | Unexpected reward when they refer | "Thanks for the referral! Here's a surprise gift." |

**When to ask for referrals (peak satisfaction moments):**
- Right after they achieve a result
- Right after they express gratitude or satisfaction
- Right after a positive support interaction
- At subscription renewal (they chose to stay = satisfied)
- After a product milestone (100th order, 1-year anniversary)

### 2. Employees

**What they are:** People within your business dedicated to lead generation -- SDRs, content creators, marketing managers, sales reps.

**Why they scale:** Once you've mastered a Core Four method yourself, you can train an employee to do it. One person doing warm outreach = 100 outreaches/day. Five people doing warm outreach = 500/day.

**The employee Lead Getter framework:**

| Step | What to Do | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **1. Document** | Write down exactly what you do | "Here's my cold email template, list criteria, and follow-up sequence" |
| **2. Demonstrate** | Show them how to do it | "Watch me send 20 cold emails, then watch me handle 5 responses" |
| **3. Delegate** | Have them do it while you watch | "Send 20 emails, I'll review before they go out" |
| **4. Distance** | Step back and manage output, not activity | "Send 100/day, report response rate weekly" |

**Key hiring principle:** Find people who can do what you're currently doing yourself. Use process checklists to systematize what works. Your goal is to transfer your knowledge, not to manage every detail.

**Roles to hire by Core Four method:**

| Core Four Method | Employee Role | What They Do |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Warm Outreach | Community Manager, Account Manager | Manage relationships, ask for referrals |
| Cold Outreach | SDR (Sales Development Rep) | Send outreach, qualify leads, book calls |
| Content | Content Creator, Video Editor | Create, edit, and publish content |
| Paid Ads | Media Buyer, Ad Manager | Create ads, manage budget, optimize campaigns |

### 3. Agencies

**What they are:** External businesses that provide lead-getting services -- ad agencies, PR firms, SEO companies, outbound sales agencies.

**The counterintuitive approach:** Hire agencies not to manage campaigns indefinitely, but to teach you their lead-getting methods. The agency agreement should focus on knowledge transfer.

**How to use agencies strategically:**

| Approach | What It Looks Like | Outcome |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Traditional** (avoid) | "Run our ads forever" | Permanent dependency, no internal capability |
| **Strategic** (recommended) | "Run our ads for 90 days AND teach our team how to do it" | Internal capability built, agency becomes optional |

**Agency evaluation criteria:**
- Do they have proof of results for businesses like yours?
- Will they teach you what they're doing (not just do it)?
- Do they specialize in your channel (not "everything")?
- What happens if you cancel -- do you keep the systems they built?
- Are they willing to do a short engagement focused on knowledge transfer?

### 4. Affiliates

**What they are:** Independent businesses or individuals who promote your offerings to their audiences in exchange for a commission. They are the most scalable Lead Getter because they bring their own audience, their own content, and their own trust.

**Hormozi's affiliate results:** Generated over $75 million in sales through affiliates without spending on advertising.

**Three-tier commission structure:**

| Tier | Commission | Trigger | Example (if CAC = $40) |
|------|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| **Tier 1** (25% of CAC) | Base rate for anyone who agrees | Signs up as affiliate | $10 per order |
| **Tier 2** (50% of CAC) | Increased rate when they activate | Makes first sale | $20 per order |
| **Tier 3** (100% of CAC) | Maximum rate for sustained performance | Consistent volume | $40 per order |

**Pay-to-play affiliate model:** Have affiliates pay to join the program. Charge $4,000-$8,000 (or 10-20% of what they're expected to make in year one). This filters for serious partners and eliminates tire-kickers.

**Why pay-to-play works:**
- Filters for people who are serious about promoting
- Creates commitment (they've invested, so they'll work harder)
- Funds your affiliate onboarding and support
- Attracts business-minded partners, not freebie seekers

**White-label lead magnets:** Create lead magnets that affiliates can rebrand and use with their own audience. This is a low-friction way to activate affiliates because you do the hard work (creating the asset) and they do the distribution.

## The Dream 100

Adapted from Chet Holmes, the Dream 100 is a list of your 100 ideal affiliate partners -- people and businesses who already have your target audience.

**How to build your Dream 100:**

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **1. Identify** | List 100 people/businesses with your target audience | "Podcasters, YouTubers, newsletter writers in our niche" |
| **2. Follow** | Consume and engage with their content | Like, comment, share their posts for 30+ days |
| **3. Give** | Provide value before asking for anything | Send a testimonial, share their content, make an introduction |
| **4. Connect** | Reach out personally with a specific compliment | "Your episode about [topic] changed how I think about [thing]" |
| **5. Propose** | Make a clear, win-win partnership offer | "I'd love to provide a free [lead magnet] for your audience. You earn $[X] for every customer." |
| **6. Support** | Make it easy for them to promote | Provide copy, graphics, landing pages, tracking links |
| **7. Celebrate** | Publicly celebrate their results | "Our partner [Name] drove 50 new customers this month!" |

**Treat affiliate acquisition like customer acquisition.** Your affiliates have an avatar (who makes a good partner), a pipeline (Dream 100), and a conversion process (relationship → proposal → activation). Apply the same rigor to recruiting affiliates as you do to acquiring customers.

## Core Four on Steroids

The ultimate scaling framework: your Lead Getters execute the Core Four on your behalf.

| | **One-to-One** | **One-to-Many** |
|---|---|---|
| **You do it** | Warm Outreach (you DM people) | Content (you post videos) |
| **Lead Getters do it** | Cold Outreach (SDRs, agencies) + Customer Referrals | Paid Ads (media buyers, agencies) + Affiliates (their content + their ads) |

**The progression:**
1. **Phase 1:** You do one Core Four method yourself
2. **Phase 2:** You master it and add a second method
3. **Phase 3:** You hire employees/agencies to do your first method while you do the second
4. **Phase 4:** All four methods running, some by you, some by Lead Getters
5. **Phase 5:** Lead Getters executing all four methods. You manage and optimize.
