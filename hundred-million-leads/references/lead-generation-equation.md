# The Lead Generation Equation: Know Your Numbers

Lead generation is not luck -- it is math. The businesses that grow fastest are not the ones with the best marketing ideas. They are the ones that know their numbers and can make rational decisions about where to invest time and money. This reference covers the complete equation, key metrics, ratio benchmarks, the client-financed acquisition model, and unit economics exercises.

## The Core Equation

**More Stuff x Better Stuff = More Leads**

"Stuff" = any form of value you give away free that demonstrates expertise before you ask for money. Content, samples, tools, webinars, experiences. The more you give (volume) and the better what you give is (quality), the more people will want what you sell.

But generating leads is only half the equation. You need to know whether those leads are profitable.

## Key Metrics

### Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)

LTGP is all the money a customer ever spends on your stuff minus all the money it takes to deliver it.

**For physical products / one-time purchases:**
```
LTGP = Average Gross Profit per Transaction x Average Number of Customer Transactions
```

**For recurring revenue / subscriptions:**
```
LTGP = Gross Profit per Customer per Period / Churn Rate
```

**Examples:**

| Business Type | Revenue per Customer | Cost to Deliver | Gross Profit | Avg Transactions | LTGP |
|--------------|---------------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|------|
| E-commerce (consumable) | $50/order | $15/order | $35/order | 6 orders | $210 |
| SaaS | $100/month | $20/month | $80/month | 18 months (5.5% churn) | $1,440 |
| Coaching program | $5,000 | $1,000 | $4,000 | 1.3 programs | $5,200 |
| Agency retainer | $3,000/month | $1,500/month | $1,500/month | 12 months | $18,000 |
| Online course | $997 | $50 | $947 | 1.5 courses | $1,420 |

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC is the total marketing and sales expenses divided by the number of new customers acquired in that period.

```
CAC = Total Marketing + Sales Expenses / Number of New Customers Acquired
```

**What to include in CAC:**
- Ad spend
- Sales team salaries and commissions
- Marketing team salaries
- Tools and software for marketing/sales
- Content creation costs
- Agency fees
- Affiliate commissions

**What NOT to include:**
- Product development costs
- Delivery/fulfillment costs (those go in LTGP calculation)
- General overhead (rent, utilities)

### The LTGP:CAC Ratio

The ratio of Lifetime Gross Profit to Customer Acquisition Cost determines whether your advertising is profitable and how aggressively you can scale.

**Target ratios:**

| Business Type | Minimum Ratio | Why |
|--------------|--------------|-----|
| No human interaction (SaaS self-serve, e-commerce) | **3:1** | Lower margins, need efficiency |
| Some human interaction (sales calls, onboarding) | **6:1** | Human costs are variable and grow with scale |
| Multiple human touchpoints (agency, coaching, consulting) | **9:1+** | High-touch delivery is expensive to scale |

**What the ratios mean:**

| Ratio | Assessment | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Below 1:1 | Losing money on every customer | Stop spending. Fix the offer, pricing, or delivery cost |
| 1:1 to 2:1 | Breaking even or thin margin | Optimize: reduce CAC, increase pricing, or improve retention |
| 3:1 | Healthy for low-touch businesses | Maintain and scale cautiously |
| 6:1 | Strong for mid-touch businesses | Scale aggressively on best channels |
| 9:1+ | Excellent for high-touch businesses | Maximum scale. Consider adding channels. |
| 20:1+ | Under-investing in growth | Spend more on acquisition -- you're leaving growth on the table |

## Client-Financed Acquisition

**Core principle:** The customer should pay back more than the cost to acquire and fulfill them within the first 30 days. This means your growth is funded by revenue, not savings or investment.

**Why 30 days?** Credit card billing cycles. If you acquire a customer today on a credit card, you typically have 30-60 days before that charge hits your statement. If the customer's first payment covers your acquisition cost within that window, you can reinvest immediately.

**How to achieve it:**

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **High first payment** | Charge enough upfront to cover CAC | "$5,000 program with $1,500 CAC = funded on day 1" |
| **Front-loaded payment plans** | Make the first payment larger | "$997 total: $497 today, then 2x $250 monthly" |
| **Immediate upsell** | Offer a complementary product at checkout | "$200 course + $97 template pack upsell = $297 day-1 revenue" |
| **Fast onboarding to value** | Get the customer using the product fast so they don't cancel | "First dashboard in 5 minutes → confirms the purchase decision" |

**Client-financed acquisition math:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average first-30-day revenue per customer | $_____ |
| Average first-30-day cost to deliver | $_____ |
| First-30-day gross profit | $_____ |
| Customer Acquisition Cost | $_____ |
| **Surplus (or deficit)** | **$_____** |

If the surplus is positive, you have client-financed acquisition. Every new customer funds the acquisition of the next customer.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: LTGP Deep Dive
Calculate your LTGP for three customer segments (e.g., by acquisition channel, by product, by cohort). Which segment has the highest LTGP? That is where you should focus your advertising.

### Exercise 2: CAC by Channel
Break down your CAC by acquisition channel (paid ads, cold email, referrals, content). Which channel has the lowest CAC? Scale that one first (More, Better, New).

### Exercise 3: 30-Day Payback Test
For your last 20 customers, calculate how much gross profit each generated in their first 30 days. What percentage covered their acquisition cost? If less than 50%, redesign your front-end offer to generate more first-30-day revenue.

### Exercise 4: The Scale Calculator
If your LTGP:CAC ratio is 6:1 and you spend an additional $10,000/month on acquisition, you will generate $60,000 in additional LTGP. What would that mean for your business? At what spend level does the ratio start to degrade? Find the scaling ceiling.
