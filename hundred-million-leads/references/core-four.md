# The Core Four: The Only Four Ways to Get Leads

Every advertising method that has ever existed is a variation of one of four methods. Email marketing, social media, billboards, word of mouth, PR, podcast interviews, webinars, trade shows -- all of them map to one of the Core Four. Understanding this simplifies lead generation from an overwhelming landscape of tactics into a focused set of four skills to master.

## The 2x2 Matrix

The Core Four are organized along two dimensions:

**Dimension 1: Audience Type**
- **Warm Audience** = people who have given you permission to contact them (friends, family, followers, current customers, past customers, contacts)
- **Cold Audience** = people who have NOT given you permission to contact them (strangers, other people's audiences)

**Dimension 2: Communication Type**
- **One-to-One** = personal, direct communication to a single person
- **One-to-Many** = broadcast communication to many people at once

| | **One-to-One** | **One-to-Many** |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm Audience** | **1. Warm Outreach** | **2. Posting Free Content** |
| **Cold Audience** | **3. Cold Outreach** | **4. Paid Ads** |

## The Four Methods Compared

| Method | Cost | Speed to Results | Scalability | Skill Required | Best Starting Point When... |
|--------|------|-----------------|-------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Warm Outreach** | Free | Immediate (days) | Low (limited by network size) | Low | You have more time than money |
| **Posting Content** | Free | Slow (weeks-months) | High (compounds over time) | Medium | You want long-term compounding |
| **Cold Outreach** | Low | Fast (1-2 weeks) | High (limited only by list size) | Medium-High | You have a clear ICP and can build lists |
| **Paid Ads** | High | Immediate (hours) | Highest | High | You have more money than time |

## Method Selection Criteria

### Start With One

Do NOT try all four at once. Pick the one that matches your current situation:

**Decision matrix:**

| If you have... | Start with... | Because... |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Time but no money | Warm Outreach | Free, immediate, builds from existing relationships |
| Time and some money | Cold Outreach | Low cost, high volume, proactive targeting |
| Money but limited time | Paid Ads | Buy attention instantly, test at speed |
| An existing audience | Posting Content | Leverage existing trust, compound what you have |
| Nothing (brand new) | Warm Outreach | Everyone knows someone. Start there. |

### When to Add the Second Method

Add a second Core Four method only when:
1. Your first method is producing consistent leads (not just occasionally)
2. You've applied "More" and "Better" to maximize it
3. You have the bandwidth (time or money) to execute a second method without degrading the first
4. You've been doing the first method for at least 30 days consistently

### The Scaling Sequence

The typical progression for most businesses:

**Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**
- Pick one Core Four method
- Apply Rule of 100 daily
- Create your first lead magnet
- Track all metrics

**Phase 2: Optimization (Months 3-6)**
- Apply More, Better, New to your first method
- Add a second Core Four method
- Refine lead magnet based on data
- Begin asking for customer referrals

**Phase 3: Scale (Months 6-12)**
- Master two Core Four methods
- Add first Lead Getter channel (customer referrals or affiliates)
- Consider third Core Four method
- Systemize and document everything

**Phase 4: Multiplication (Months 12+)**
- All four Core Four methods active (some may be managed by others)
- Multiple Lead Getter channels active
- Core Four on Steroids: Lead Getters executing the Core Four
- Focus shifts from doing to managing and optimizing

## How the Core Four Work Together

The methods are not independent -- they reinforce each other:

**Content warms cold audiences:**
- Cold outreach recipient checks your profile → sees great content → responds
- Paid ad viewer clicks to your page → sees valuable posts → opts in

**Warm outreach generates content:**
- Conversations reveal pain points → content topics
- Customer success stories → case study content

**Paid ads accelerate organic:**
- Content that works organically → becomes paid ad creative
- Paid ads build audience → organic content reaches more people

**Cold outreach feeds warm outreach:**
- Cold outreach converts strangers to followers → warm audience grows
- Cold outreach generates customers → customers become referral sources

## Channel Mastery Checklist

Use this checklist to assess whether you've mastered a Core Four method before adding another:

### Warm Outreach Mastery
- [ ] Completed 100-day Rule of 100 commitment
- [ ] Reached out to 1,000+ contacts
- [ ] Have a repeatable script/framework (ACA)
- [ ] Consistently generating 5+ engaged leads per week
- [ ] Getting referrals from warm conversations
- [ ] Have documented the process so someone else could do it

### Content Mastery
- [ ] Posting consistently for 90+ days
- [ ] Using Hook-Retain-Reward framework
- [ ] Generating inbound leads/DMs from content
- [ ] Know your top-performing content formats
- [ ] Have a content creation system (batch creation, templates)
- [ ] Audience is growing month over month

### Cold Outreach Mastery
- [ ] Sending 100+ outreaches per day consistently
- [ ] Response rate above 1%
- [ ] Have a qualified lead list of 10,000+
- [ ] Personalization system in place (not copy-paste)
- [ ] Follow-up sequence running automatically
- [ ] Generating 10+ engaged leads per week

### Paid Ads Mastery
- [ ] Spending $100+/day consistently
- [ ] LTGP:CAC ratio at 3:1 or better
- [ ] Have 3+ winning ad creatives
- [ ] Testing new hooks weekly
- [ ] Tracking cost per lead and cost per acquisition
- [ ] Can scale spend without ratio degradation

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Core Four Audit
For each of the four methods, rate your current execution 1-10. Which is your strongest? Which is non-existent? The gap between your strongest and weakest is your biggest opportunity.

### Exercise 2: Method Selection
Based on your current resources (time, money, audience), which Core Four method should you start with or double down on? Write a 100-day commitment plan for that method.

### Exercise 3: Reinforcement Map
Draw the four methods in a square. For each pair, write one sentence about how they reinforce each other. Where can you create feedback loops between methods?

### Exercise 4: Team Mapping
For each Core Four method, write who is currently responsible. If the answer is "me" for all four, you have a scaling problem. Which method should you delegate first?
