# Buyer Profile Template

Use this structure for every buyer profile. Every section is required. Do not skip or merge sections.

---

## 1. Demographic Snapshot

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Name** | Give the avatar a realistic first name |
| **Age** | Age or age range |
| **Brief Description** | 2-3 sentences capturing who this person is, what their day looks like, and where they are in life |

---

## 2. Core Problem

**The central, dominating issue in our prospect's life.** This is the urgent, pressing problem they're currently dealing with — the thing the product/service is designed to solve.

Write it as a single, punchy statement. Then expand with 2-3 sentences of context.

### Top 5 Most Powerful Emotions Around That Problem

List the five dominant emotions the prospect feels when confronting this problem. Not clinical labels — describe the feeling the way the prospect would internally experience it.

### Top 5 Biggest Fears

The deepest fears related to this problem that the prospect would never admit out loud. These are 3AM fears — dark, catastrophic, worst-case scenarios the prospect privately imagines even if unrealistic. Explore the darkness. These are never shared with the prospect.

For each fear, write it as a raw internal monologue statement.

### 5 Ways Those Fears Affect Key Relationships

For each fear, describe how it specifically impacts a named relationship in the prospect's life (spouse, child, parent, friend, coworker, in-law, competitor). Be vivid, descriptive, and emotional. Show the scene, don't summarize it.

### 5 Conversational But Hurtful Things Those Relationships Might Say

Quotes from specific people in the prospect's life — labeled by relationship. Include:
- Well-meaning supporters who say unintentionally triggering things
- Antagonists (judgmental relatives, competitors, doubters)

Format each as:

> **[Relationship]:** "Quote here."

---

## 3. Other Solutions

### What Our Prospects Have Tried in the Past (5 Solutions)

List 5 commonly known solutions/approaches the target market has already attempted. These should be widely recognized within the market.

### Brief Conversational Soundbites About Failed Past Solutions

For each failed solution, write a raw soundbite in the prospect's voice — frustration, disappointment, regret.

> "Quote capturing their experience with [solution]."

### What Our Prospects Don't Want to Do to Fix Their Problem (5 Items)

Tasks they refuse to do. Sacrifices they won't make. Risks they won't take. Things they've sworn off.

### Brief Conversational Soundbites About What They Don't Want to Do

For each item, write an internal monologue soundbite.

> "Quote capturing their resistance."

---

## 4. Primary Transformation

### Magic Genie Solution — 5 Dimensionalized Outcomes

If a genie could snap their fingers and give the prospect the perfect solution, what would their life look like? List 5 specific, vivid outcomes written in the prospect's own language. Become the prospect. Paint the picture.

### How Would This Transformation Affect Emotions, Activities, Relationships?

Assuming the genie granted all wishes — how does this impact:
- **Confidence & self-image**
- **Respect & reputation**
- **Daily activities & lifestyle**
- **Relationships & social dynamics**
- **Status, sex appeal, how others view/treat them**

This is the prospect's fantasy. It's allowed to be vain, superficial, and aspirational. We're uncovering emotional drivers they'd never admit.

### How Would This Transformation Affect Key Relationships?

Describe specific changes in how named relationships would shift. More respect from the spouse. Admiration from the sibling who always doubted them. Jealousy from the competitor.

### Specific Post-Transformation Soundbites

What does the prospect want people to say to them after the transformation? Include:
- Supporters who are proud and impressed
- Doubters/antagonists who are forced to admit they were wrong, are jealous, or come begging for advice

Format each as:

> **[Relationship]:** "Quote here."

---

## 5. Market Specifics

### What Does the Market Hinge Their Success On?

The pre-existing belief about what condition must be met to achieve results. What does the prospect believe is the KEY to everything? (e.g., "I just need more traffic," "I just need to find the right supplier," "If I could just speed up my metabolism...")

### What Does the Market Have to Give Up By Giving Up Their Problem?

The hidden psychological comfort or identity the prospect gains from keeping their problem. What secondary benefit does the problem provide? (e.g., an excuse not to try, fuel for righteous anger, permission to feel sorry for themselves, a reason to stay in their comfort zone)

### Who Does the Market Blame For Their Problem?

Both internal and external factors:
- **External:** Government, corporations, platforms, gurus, economy, competitors, algorithms
- **Internal (perceived):** "I'm not tech-savvy enough," "I don't have the right connections," "I started too late"

### Top 5 Biggest Objections the Market Might Have

The reasons prospects give for not buying or not taking action. Frame as the prospect would say them.
