# Interview Questions

Use these questions when the user hasn't provided enough context about their product, market, or avatar. Don't ask all questions at once — group them into rounds of 3-5 and adapt based on answers.

## Round 1: Product & Market Foundation

Start here. You need to understand what they sell and who they sell it to.

1. What is your product or service? (If TBD, that's fine — we'll profile the market first)
2. Who is your target market? Describe them in plain language.
3. What problem does your product/service solve for them?

## Round 2: Problem Deep-Dive

Once you know the market, dig into the problem.

4. How would you describe the main problem or challenge your prospects face?
5. To what extent do your prospects feel this problem is within their control?
6. How often do they experience this problem? (Daily? Weekly? Situational?)
7. How comfortable are they discussing this problem with others? (1-10)
8. How would they describe this problem to a close friend or family member?
9. What specific aspects of their lives have been most affected?
10. How has this problem impacted their self-confidence and self-image?
11. Besides the prospect, who is most directly affected by this problem?
12. What does a typical day/week look like while dealing with this problem vs. their ideal?
13. What is their biggest fear if this problem doesn't get better?

## Round 3: Solution & Transformation

14. If they could wave a magic wand and solve this problem, what would their life look like?
15. What are the top 3 other benefits they hope to gain by resolving this?
16. What would their closest loved ones say if they successfully overcame this?
17. What would it mean for their sense of identity to solve this?

## Round 4: Past Experience

18. What solutions or approaches have they tried in the past? What were the outcomes?
19. What did they like or dislike about previous solutions?
20. How long have they been actively trying to solve this? How has their approach evolved?
21. What lessons have they learned from past attempts? What would they do differently?

## Round 5: Beliefs & Constraints

22. How knowledgeable do they feel about the underlying causes of their problem?
23. What beliefs do they hold about the ideal solution?
24. What has prevented them from solving this so far?
25. What resources or support do they feel they need?
26. What do they feel is the missing piece to finally achieving a lasting solution?
27. What unique challenges exist at this stage of their life?
28. How much time and energy can they realistically invest per week?

## Interview Tips

- **Listen for emotion** — when the user describes something with heat, follow that thread
- **Ask "why" one more time** — surface answers hide deeper drivers
- **Capture their exact words** — the user's phrasing often mirrors the prospect's language
- **Don't rush** — a thorough interview produces a dramatically better profile
- **Adapt** — skip questions you've already answered, drill into gaps
