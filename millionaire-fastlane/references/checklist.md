# The Fastlane Checklist — CENTS Scoring + 40 Lifestyle Guidelines

## Part 1 — The CENTS Scorecard

Use this worksheet to audit any business idea, wealth plan, or career move. Score each commandment 0/1 (fail/pass). The goal is 5/5. Violations do not disqualify, but they degrade Fastlane potential — use the "severity" column to decide whether to proceed, restructure, or walk away.

### The business / path being scored

- **Name or description:** ___________
- **Who the customer is:** ___________
- **What specific need it solves:** ___________
- **What you charge and what your cost is:** ___________
- **How many units you can realistically sell:** ___________

### CENTS screen

| # | Commandment | Pass criterion | Score | Notes |
|---|-------------|---------------|-------|-------|
| 1 | **Control** | Can I change product, price, marketing, revenue model, and compensation without another party's vote? | 0/1 | |
| 2 | **Entry** | Is starting this a multi-step *process* with real barriers, not an *event* anyone can replicate in minutes? | 0/1 | |
| 3 | **Need** | Does this solve a specific pain or unmet need for someone other than me? | 0/1 | |
| 4 | **Time** | Can the business run for a week without me physically present? | 0/1 | |
| 5 | **Scale** | Can Net Profit credibly grow from $2K/mo to $200K/mo via reach and/or magnitude? | 0/1 | |

**Total:** ___ / 5

### Interpretation

- **5/5** — Potent Fastlane road. One of the "Three Interstates" (Internet, Innovation, Intentional Iteration) [MF p. 243].
- **4/5** — Viable with restructuring. Identify the violated commandment and design a fix.
- **3/5** — Significant wealth potential degradation. DeMarco: "As violations accrue, the road degrades in its wealth potential." [MF p. 206]
- **2/5 or lower** — Likely a Slowlane business masquerading as entrepreneurship (job in founder costume). Walk or redesign.

### Restructuring fixes for common failures

- **Control fail (hitchhiking)** — become the driver. Found the platform; sell the franchise; offer the affiliate program; employ instead of employed.
- **Entry fail ("everyone is doing it")** — raise barriers via capital, regulation, IP, brand, or process; OR commit to exceptionality (top 0.1% execution).
- **Need fail (selfish premise)** — reframe around customer pain. Interview 10 potential customers about their actual problems before committing.
- **Time fail (job in disguise)** — add a money-tree seedling (content, computer, software, distribution, HR). If none can be added, the road is structurally wrong.
- **Scale fail (tiny habitat)** — expand habitat (local → regional → national → worldwide) or raise magnitude (unit profit × 10).

## Part 2 — The 10-Step Fastlane Process

A sequential action plan derived from DeMarco's Ch. 45 synthesis. Work through these in order; do not skip ahead.

1. **Admit the Slowlane is flawed and swap roadmaps.** [MF p. 310]
2. **Reflect on past choices and adopt decision tools — Worst-Case Consequence Analysis (WCCA) + Weighted Average Decision Matrix (WADM).** [MF pp. 166–171]
3. **Remove human and environmental headwinds; reclaim time by killing parasitic debt.** [MF pp. 175–185]
4. **Commit to lifelong education and hit the Redline — "Someday is today."** [MF p. 201]
5. **Uncouple from the Slowlane by forming a corporate entity (C-corp, S-corp, or LLC).** [MF pp. 151–153]
6. **Set a destination — lifestyle target, cost per year, money-system target, business-system target.** [MF pp. 256–258]
7. **Find an open road — a pain/need that satisfies all five CENTS commandments.** [MF pp. 206, 250–253]
8. **Burn the business plan and execute — put the product in the box; let the market steer.** [MF pp. 271–273]
9. **Grow: exceed expectations (SUCS), verify-before-trust, build a USP/brand, stay monogamous to ONE business.** [MF pp. 278–307]
10. **Exit via liquidation event — convert asset value to money-system capital. Retire, reward, or repeat.** [MF p. 313]

## Part 4 — The "Three Interstates" (5/5 business categories)

When starting fresh and looking for a 5/5 road, focus on DeMarco's three umbrella categories [MF p. 243]:

1. **Internet** — lead-generation, subscription, brokerage, content, e-commerce, SaaS, ad networks.
2. **Innovation** — inventing products, repackaging existing products better (Snuggie, Crystal Head vodka), branding to solve unmet emotional need.
3. **Intentional Iteration** — chaining / franchising / replicating a proven unit (Starbucks, McDonald's, Dogfish Head as brewery expansion).

Money-tree seedling types that can grow under any Interstate: content, computer, software, distribution, human-resource systems [MF p. 241].
