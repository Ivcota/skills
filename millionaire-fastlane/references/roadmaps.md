# The Three Roadmaps — Deep Dive

> "Self-made millionaires don't become millionaires by stumbling into money, just as financial failures don't become failures by stumbling into poorness. Both are the direct result of the financial roadmap chosen and the actions and beliefs that evolve from that roadmap." — [MF Ch. 4, p. 26]

DeMarco's central metaphor. Wealth is a road trip, not a road. The *roadmap* — your belief system, wealth equation, and control posture — determines your outcome. Three roadmaps compete for every financial life.

> "Like Einstein's E=MC², these formulas govern your wealth universe much like physics governs our universe." — [MF Ch. 4, p. 28]

## The 11 mindposts

DeMarco audits each roadmap across 11 dimensions [MF Ch. 4, p. 28]:

1. Debt Perception
2. Time Perception
3. Education Perception
4. Money Perception
5. Primary Income Source
6. Primary Wealth Accelerator
7. Wealth Perception
8. Wealth Equation
9. Destination
10. Responsibility & Control
11. Life Perception

Each roadmap has a distinct signature across all 11. Run the user's self-assessment against this matrix to locate them.

## The Sidewalk — "Poorness"

**Concept:** No plan, instant gratification, wealth = income + debt. Perpetually "one-something-from-broke" regardless of income level.

**Mechanism:** Surplus income gets spent on the next gadget, trip, or fad. Debt supplements lifestyle beyond income. Any bump (layoff, recession, injury) collapses the illusion into bankruptcy.

**Wealth equation:** `Wealth = Income + Debt` [MF p. 39]

**Mindpost signatures** [MF pp. 34–35]:
- Debt: "Credit allows me to buy things now! If I want it now, I'm going to get it now."
- Time: "Time is abundant and I spend money like there's no tomorrow."
- Education: "I finished school when I graduated, hooray!"
- Money: "If you got it, flaunt it! Why save for a rainy day?"
- Wealth: "He who dies with the most toys wins!"
- Accelerator: Casinos, lottery tickets, lawsuits.
- Responsibility: "The man is keeping me down. I am a victim. It's someone else's fault."
- Life: "Live today, to hell with tomorrow."
- Destination: "What destination? I live for today."

**Three anchoring Sidewalk beliefs** [MF p. 54]:
1. Luck is needed for wealth.
2. Wealth is an event.
3. Others can give wealth to me.

**Outcome trajectory:** Net worth stays ~zero or negative even as income rises; ends in bankruptcy or crisis of reckoning [MF pp. 33, 38].

**Income-Rich Sidewalker trap:** High income does not exempt you. Pro athletes, rappers, actors go broke the same way income-poor Sidewalkers do. The constraint is money management, not money [MF p. 39]. DeMarco cites Nicolas Cage losing $20M to his business manager [MF p. 261] and Callie, the UK 16-year-old who won $3M and blew it in 6 years on drugs, breast implants, and $730K of clothing [MF p. 101].

**Faux wealth** — the Ch. 6 concept [MF pp. 42–44]: financing luxury to impersonate wealth destroys the trinity of real wealth (family, fitness, freedom). Lifestyle servitude locks you into the job-income-debt loop.

## The Slowlane — "Mediocrity"

**Concept:** The socially-sanctioned "Get Rich Slow" plan. Trade 40–50 years of a job for frugality, market investments, and a deferred retirement.

**Mechanism:** Income comes from a job (intrinsic value). Wealth is "accelerated" by compound interest on mutual funds and 401K contributions. Both variables are time-bound, capped, and uncontrollable. DeMarco names this Uncontrollable Limited Leverage (ULL) [MF pp. 78–82].

**Wealth equation:** `Wealth = (Job) + (Market Investments) = Intrinsic Value + Compound Interest` [MF pp. 67, 79]

Factored: `Wealth = (Time × Hourly or Salaried Value) + Invested Sum × (1 + Yield)^Time` [MF p. 81]

**Mindpost signatures** [MF p. 66]:
- Debt: "Debt is evil. It must be religiously attacked, even if that means working overtime for life."
- Time: "My time is abundant and I will gladly trade my time for more dollars."
- Education: "Education is important because it helps me earn a bigger salary."
- Money: "Money is scarce and every dime must be accounted for, budgeted, and perilously saved."
- Wealth: "Work, save, and invest. Repeat for 40 years."
- Destination: "A comfortable retirement in my twilight years."
- Responsibility: "I have to rely on others — employer, financial adviser, government, economy."
- Life: "Settle for less. Give up on big dreams. Save, live frugal, don't take unnecessary risks."

**Slowlane buzz phrases** [MF p. 67]: "Pay yourself first / Contribute to your 401K / Invest in mutual funds / Buy and hold / Diversify / Say 'no' to expensive lattes / Be frugal / Clip coupons / Pay off your house early / Your home is an asset / Dollar cost averaging / Understand compound interest."

**Six structural "job suckages"** [MF Ch. 11, pp. 74–77]:
1. Time-for-life trade.
2. Skill limitation.
3. No control.
4. Office politics.
5. "Pay yourself last" via taxation.
6. Dictatorship on income.

**The 5-for-2 trade** [MF p. 69]: trading 5 days of work-bondage for 2 days of freedom is a **–60% return** on time.

**Caps on the Slowlane equation**:
- Intrinsic value caps you at hours × rate or annual salary [MF p. 80].
- Compound interest needs time, yield, and sum — and none can be leveraged or controlled [MF p. 82].
- Market yield of 8% average masks drawdowns of 40–50% in a single year [MF p. 81].
- Only ~10% of penta-millionaires (net worth $5M+) report their wealth came from passive market investments [MF p. 83].

> "The plan is a failure because the plan is based on time and factors you can't control." — [MF p. 65]

**Outcome trajectory:** 40–50 year linear trudge. Wealth — if realized — arrives at 65, paired with lost health and youth. "The Slowlane is predisposed to mediocrity. Life isn't great, but it isn't so bad either." [MF p. 71]

## The Fastlane — "Wealth"

**Concept:** A business-and-lifestyle roadmap defined by Controllable Unlimited Leverage (CUL). A producer builds a system whose net profit and asset value grow rapidly without the time cap of a job.

**Mechanism:** Income is generated by a business system (not by selling hours). Wealth accumulates via two controllable, unlimited variables — net profit and asset value — both of which can be manipulated upward and converted to cash via liquidation [MF Ch. 16–18].

**Wealth equation:** `Wealth = Net Profit + Asset Value` [MF p. 110]

Factored: `Net Profit = Units Sold × Unit Profit`; `Asset Value = Net Profit × Industry Multiplier` [MF pp. 121, 123]

**Mindpost signatures** [MF pp. 109–110]:
- Debt: "Debt is useful if it allows me to build and grow my system."
- Time: "Time is the most important asset I have, far exceeding money."
- Education: "The moment you stop learning is the moment you stop growing."
- Money: "Money is everywhere, and it's extremely abundant. Money is a reflection of how many lives I've touched."
- Accelerator: "I make something from nothing. I give birth to assets and make them valuable to the marketplace."
- Wealth: "Build business systems for cash flow and asset valuation."
- Destination: "Lifetime passive income, either through business or investments."
- Strategy: "The more I help, the richer I become in time, money, and personal fulfillment."
- Life: "My dreams are worth pursuing no matter how outlandish."
- Team allegiance: "Become a producer first and a consumer second." [MF p. 117]

**The Chuma-Azur parable** [MF pp. 112–115]: two brothers tasked with building a pyramid. Azur starts hauling bricks manually. Chuma spends years building a machine. By year 5, Chuma's machine moves more stone per day than Azur has moved in a lifetime. Fastlane = build the machine, then let the machine build the pyramid.

**Team switching** [MF Ch. 17, p. 117]: "Instead of digging for gold, sell shovels. Instead of taking a class, offer a class. Instead of borrowing money, lend it." Every consumer action has a producer inverse — the Fastlane move is to invert.

**Wealth Acceleration Factor**: ~1,700% for Fastlane vs. ~8% for Slowlane market investor [MF p. 125]. Liquidation events compress years of appreciation into a single cash moment.

> "The risk profile of a Fastlane strategy isn't much different from the Slowlane, but the rewards are far greater." — [MF p. 107]

**Get Rich Quick vs. Get Rich Easy** [MF p. 111]: DeMarco rehabilitates "Get Rich Quick" as real — rapid wealth is achievable via process. What's fake is "Get Rich Easy" — the guru promise of wealth without work. Process comes before event.

**Outcome trajectory:** Rapid, exponential wealth compressed into a few years; culminates in a liquidation event, followed by lifetime passive income and reclaimed time. Wealth redefined around the **three F's — family, fitness, freedom** [MF Ch. 6, p. 42].

## Switching roadmaps

The Fastlane transition, per DeMarco [MF Ch. 45, pp. 309–313]:
1. **Admit** the Slowlane is flawed.
2. **Stop & Swap** — stop consuming; swap allegiances from consumer to producer.
3. **Steer** your choices with WCCA (Worst-Case Consequence Analysis) and WADM (Weighted Average Decision Matrix) [MF Ch. 23].
4. **Uncouple** by forming a C-corp, S-corp, or LLC [MF Ch. 22].
5. **Remove headwinds** (dream-stealing relationships, parasitic debt) [MF Ch. 25–26].
6. **Find a road** that satisfies CENTS.
7. **Execute** — burn the business plan, put the product in the box [MF Ch. 39].
