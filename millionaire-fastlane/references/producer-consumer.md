# Producer vs. Consumer — Deep Dive

> "Producers get rich. Consumers get poor. Switch teams and reorient as a producer first, a consumer second." — [MF Ch. 17, p. 118]

The Producer/Consumer dichotomy is DeMarco's identity-level shift. It is upstream of every commandment: until you stop seeing the world as a consumer, the CENTS framework reads like academic rules. Once you switch teams, it reads like a playbook.

## The team-switch thesis

> "From the day you were born, you were baptized to play for Team Consumer, from the Barbie Doll and the Tonka Truck to the Star Wars action figures. You've been conditioned to demand: to want products, to need products, to buy products, and of course, to seek out the cheapest of those products." [MF p. 116]

Slowlane jobs exist to *facilitate the consumer process*. The brand manager, the retail-store accountant, the insurance agent — all roles in moving goods to consumers. This consumer focus is "a gravitational pull to keep you amenable to anti-Fastlane thinking" [MF p. 116].

> "Decoding the Fastlane roadmap is as simple as joining the team that is custodian to the decryption key. The winning team is Team Producer. Reshape life's focus on producing, not consuming." [MF p. 117]

## The six reorientations

DeMarco's verbatim list [MF p. 117]:

| Consumer move | Producer move |
|---|---|
| Buy products on TV | **Sell** products |
| Dig for gold | **Sell shovels** |
| Take a class | **Offer** a class |
| Borrow money | **Lend** money |
| Take a job | **Hire** for jobs |
| Take a mortgage | **Hold** a mortgage |

The structural pattern: in every transaction, ask which side captures the margin. The producer side does. Always sit there.

## Producer Reorientation as a daily practice

When encountering any advertising message, sales pitch, or product, run DeMarco's questions [MF p. 117]:

1. How does this company make money?
2. What is the aim of its message?
3. What kind of business processes are involved in offering this product or service?
4. Is this company making a profit?
5. What is the revenue model?
6. Is this product manufactured overseas or locally?

> "I've never bought a product on late night television, because I'm on the same team. As a producer, I see the infomercials for what they are: producers (the minority) serving the consumer (the majority). The 'act nows,' the 'but wait, there's more!,' the 'free bonuses' — these are marketing weapons in a producer's arsenal. I watch infomercials not to buy, but to see what the pros are doing." [MF p. 117]

> "As producers, our job is to entice consumers to buy. As a producer locked into a producer mindset, I attract wealth because consumers seek producers. Consumers are the majority who demand their fill!" [MF p. 117]

## The Money-Chaser failure mode

The most insidious trap: starting a business doesn't make you a producer. **Money chasers are consumers who haven't quite made the transition.** They want the producer's outcome but think with the consumer's selfishness — "what's in it for me?" [MF p. 210]

> "Money chasers are consumers who haven't quite made the transition to producer. They want to be producers, but they selfishly think like consumers." [MF p. 210]

DeMarco's archetypal example: the housing-bubble mortgage broker → loan modifier → next-trend operator. They show up wherever the headlines point. With "plenty of selfish consumers to prey upon, money chasers survive and thrive until the next bust or they're exposed for fraud." [MF p. 210]

**Detection cues for money-chaser thinking** in yourself or a partner:

- The business idea changes with whatever's hot this quarter
- Pricing decisions optimize for "what can I extract" not "what does this solve"
- The pitch leads with the founder's lifestyle goals, not the customer's pain
- "I love X, therefore I should sell X" (the Need violation that masks as passion)

## To consume richly, produce effectively

> "And the irony of this producer/consumer dichotomy? Once you succeed as a producer, you can consume anything you want with little consequence because you'll be rich. To consume richly, produce richly first." [MF p. 117–118]

The Fastlane is not asceticism. It's *deferred consumption with no upper bound on what you eventually consume*. The Slowlane gets it backwards: consumption restraint as a path. Restraint without producer-side wealth-building is just budgeting; you remain a consumer who consumes less.

## Team Producer attributes

From the Ch. 17 summary [MF p. 119]:

- Producers are indigenous to the Fastlane roadmap.
- Producers are the minority, as are the rich; consumers are the majority, as are the poor.
- When you succeed as a producer, you can consume anything you want.
- Fastlaners are producers, entrepreneurs, innovators, visionaries, and creators.
- A business does not make a Fastlane — some businesses are jobs in disguise.
- The Fastlane wealth equation is not bound by time and its variables are unlimited and controllable.

## Operational checklist

When evaluating any business idea, career move, or financial decision, ask:

- [ ] **Side of the table:** Am I the producer or the consumer in this transaction?
- [ ] **Margin direction:** Where does the margin flow — to me or away from me?
- [ ] **Selfishness check:** Did I start with "what does the market need" or with "what do I want to do"?
- [ ] **Boom-chasing check:** Is this idea trending because I follow trends, or because I noticed an unfilled need first?
- [ ] **Producer questions:** Can I articulate the revenue model, the customer pain, and the distribution channel?

If any of these fail, you're either still a consumer or a money-chaser. Reorient before launching.

## Connection to the commandments

Producer/Consumer is the identity layer; CENTS is the operational layer. They map:

- **Control** — Producers own; consumers participate. (Driver vs. hitchhiker.)
- **Entry** — Producers raise barriers; consumers chase low-friction entry.
- **Need** — Producers solve; consumers demand.
- **Time** — Producers automate; consumers trade hours.
- **Scale** — Producers leverage; consumers single-transact.

The acronyms describe the road; Producer/Consumer describes the driver.
